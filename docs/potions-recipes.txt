--------------------
	HERBS
--------------------

1. Moonwort (Botrychium lunarna)

A small mystical fern traditionally used in fol medicine
And lunar herbalism, associated with healing and hidden knowledge 

2. Wall Rue (Salvis vitae)

Up to 3 inches high, grows in tufts and crevices of walls. Pale-green
colored, considered good for coughs and ruptures in children.

3. Mandrake

Tubers that look like newborns, their screams can kill when fully grown.
Can bring the petrified back to life. (Harry Potter Reference)

# gameplay idea: Slows down enemies for 5 seconds after harvesting

4. Feainnewedd

A flower which , according to the legend, grows only in places where elder blood has been
Spilled, and in Dol Blathanna. (The Witcher 3 reference)

5. Poppy Flowers

A powerful liquid medicine, similar to opium; eases pain and can be used as an anesthetic.
Made with crushed poppy flowers

--------------------
   HARMFUL HERBS
--------------------

1. Poison Hemlock 

A highly toxic plant containing Connie. Causes ascending paralysis and respiratory failure 

2. Night Howler (Midnicampum holitcithias)

A purple flower that contains a toxic compound that, upon entering the bloodstream causes mammals 
To revert to a violent feral state - Zootopia reference 

3. Albert Ivy

Upon consumption causes intense neurological hallucinations. Distinguishable from regular ivy by the presence of orange petals.

~Designed by Marley Feldman 

4. Druid's Sorrow - Star Stable reference

It represents grief, tragedy and historical loses the Druid suffered fighting dark forces

4. Love Fern

A pale, moonlit herb known for its silvery leaves. Toxic when consumed, slowly draining strength and vitality over time.
Found it damp areas untouched by sunshine.

*Inspired by Scarlett Wallis and "How to lose a guy in 10 days"

5. Woolfsbane - The Witcher 3 reference 

A highly toxic herb, famous for being a core ingredient in the decoctions used during the Witcher Trials. It is also utilized in crafting lethal poisons

--------------------
       POTIONS
--------------------
### Numbers reflect the recommended usage sequence, unless we don't want to restrict the player and specify levels

1. Milk of the Poppy (Game of Thrones reference)

Strong anesthetic used during surgery in GoT -> I suggest using it first
As the first stage of healing the tree

2.  Mandrake Restorative Draught (Harry Potter Reference)

A powerful healing potion used to restore those who have been petrified or transfigured to their original state -> I suggest using it second as in other franchises it is not used primarily as a healing potion, but as a tool to revert the patient to their original state

3. Rubedo

When used in subsequent potion brewing provides an additional passive regeneration to vitality
(In The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt) -> I suggest using it 3rd as it isn't the strongest healing potion in game

4. Lunaria

In many games used as a healing potion, I recommend using it last as both the name of the potion "lunaria" and the name of the herb "moonwort" connect to the game itself (moonfern) and the name of the studio (Lunara Games)
